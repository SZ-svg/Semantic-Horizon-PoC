### Раунд 1
**A:** Within the framework of axiomatic set theory, Gödel's incompleteness theorems establish that any sufficiently powerful formal system is either incomplete or inconsistent. The recursive boundary refers to the limit beyond which the axiomatic system cannot prove its own consistency, thereby rendering it incomplete. This boundary is inherently tied to the halting problem in computability theory, where the undecidability of certain statements mirrors the incompleteness of the axiomatic system.
