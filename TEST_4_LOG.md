### Раунд 1
**A:** In a closed semiotic system, the signifier-signified mapping is bounded by the system's internal consistency and the finite number of possible signifiers and signifieds. The limit of this mapping is reached when the system's constraints, such as the rules of syntax and semantics, are exhausted, resulting in a saturation of meaning. This saturation point marks the boundary beyond which the system's ability to generate new signifier-signified relationships breaks down.
